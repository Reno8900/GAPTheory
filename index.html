What is GAP Theory?

GAP (Gradient-Activated Pressure) is a covariant, zero-free-parameter extension of Einstein's general relativity. It introduces a single new scalar field Ξ — the modular vacuum susceptibility — that responds to gradients of the baryonic stress-energy tensor rather than to absolute vacuum energy.

Core idea. The cosmological constant problem asks: why doesn't the enormous vacuum energy of quantum field theory gravitate? GAP answers this by postulating that gravity couples not to the absolute vacuum energy density, but only to its modular response — the differential susceptibility of the vacuum to baryonic stress gradients. This single shift produces MOND-like galaxy rotation without dark matter, replaces dark energy with a vacuum polarization mechanism, and yields exact analytic predictions for the BTFR, RAR, CMB sound horizon, and the cosmological constant — all from first principles, with no system-dependent free parameters.
