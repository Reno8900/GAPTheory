Coherence Phase Model

Gravity from a
coherent vacuum phase

A framework in which the quantum vacuum behaves like a phase-ordered medium. Where the vacuum is coherent, gravity appears enhanced — matching galaxy rotation curves without dark matter. Where it's not, gravity behaves normally — and clusters look as they do.

Headline claim A coherence-based phase model appears to organize dark-matter-like behavior across galaxies, groups, and clusters using a single physical variable: ρ/σ³.
See the strongest result Summary in plain English
Summary

In plain English

For decades, physics has assumed the universe is filled with invisible "dark matter" to explain why galaxies rotate the way they do. This theory takes a different approach: what if gravity itself changes depending on the environment — because it emerges from the vacuum?

01

What this is about

Instead of adding unseen matter, the theory says the vacuum of space behaves like a physical medium. Under the right conditions it forms a coherent state — like a superfluid. In that state, gravity appears stronger, matching galaxy rotation curves. In other conditions, the vacuum behaves normally, and gravity looks like the cluster-scale gravity we measure.

02

Where we started

Galaxies follow a precise law (the Radial Acceleration Relation) connecting visible matter to gravity. It is extremely consistent — and difficult to explain with standard dark-matter halos. We asked the question: can this law be derived from physics, instead of just fitted?

03

What we have

A model where (i) the galaxy law is derived from quantum statistics and horizon physics, not assumed; (ii) the universe has two phases — coherent (galaxies) and normal (clusters); (iii) galaxy groups, sitting between, reveal the transition; and (iv) the phase-ordering variable ρ/σ³ sorts systems in the right direction across all three scales.

04

Environment matters

Two physical effects shape behavior. Formation: dense, calm systems can enter the coherent phase; disturbed environments (lower density, higher motion) prevent it from forming. Survival: even where the phase forms, strong tidal forces can disrupt it. Both are partially derived and physically plausible.

05

Lensing supports it

Gravitational lensing tests don't depend on velocity measurements. Stacked KiDS+GAMA group lensing shows systems with higher predicted coherence behave more like galaxies, while lower-coherence systems behave more like dark-matter-dominated clusters — independent confirmation of the direction.

06

What's left to prove

This is not a finished theory. The vacuum response kernel is not fully derived from first principles; cs and the EFT need to be self-consistent; and the threshold needs to be validated cleanly across the galaxy → group → cluster ladder using only systems with directly measured velocity dispersions. The framework is promising; it is not yet proven.

The mechanism

How the model works

Four ingredients. Each one is either a quantum-statistical input (Bose), a horizon-physics input (Unruh, de Sitter), or a phase-ordering parameter. The framework is built to be auditable end-to-end.

01

The coherence parameter

A single variable orders galaxies, groups, and clusters.

The phase of the vacuum is set by the dimensionless coherence parameter Ccoh = n · λdB³, the product of local density and the cube of the de Broglie wavelength. Equivalently — through quantum statistics — this is set by ρ/σ³, the ratio of mass density to velocity-dispersion-cubed.

\[C_{\rm coh} = n\,\lambda_{\rm dB}^3 \;\propto\; \rho/\sigma^3\]

When Ccoh > 1, the vacuum is coherent and gravity appears MOND-like. When Ccoh < 1, it behaves normally and gravity looks CDM-like.

02

RAR from Bose + horizon temperatures

The galaxy law isn't fitted — it follows from quantum statistics.

An accelerated observer at acceleration gb sees an Unruh temperature; the de Sitter horizon contributes its own temperature set by the cosmological scale a₀. Combining these with fluctuation–dissipation and Bose occupation statistics yields the radial acceleration relation:

\[g_{\rm obs} = \frac{g_b}{1 - e^{-\sqrt{g_b/a_0}}}\]
Why this matters The RAR has been treated as a tight empirical correlation. In this model it is structural — it falls out of quantum statistics in a horizon-temperature background.
03

Two-stage environmental model

Formation and survival are separately controlled.

Formation: the coherent phase forms when local density is high and internal motion is low. The preconditioning factor multiplies Ccoh:

\[C_{\rm form} = C_{\rm coh} \cdot P_{\rm pre}, \qquad P_{\rm pre} = \frac{f_\rho}{f_\sigma^3}\]

Survival: once formed, the phase can be disrupted by tidal gradients in the external gravitational field. A dimensionless disruption parameter Denv controls survival probability Senv = e−Denv. Empirically: Siso = 0.92 vs Semb = 0.76, with p = 0.018.

04

Lensing as a clean probe

No velocity dispersions needed.

Stacked weak-lensing measurements around galaxy groups in KiDS+GAMA give mass estimates that are σ-independent. The model predicts the direction of the mass excess from the coherence variable alone:

\[\rho_S(\log C,\,\log M_{\rm excess}) \;=\; -0.94, \quad p = 7\times 10^{-10}\]

The lensing direction is the strongest single piece of evidence for the model — and crucially, it does not depend on the velocity measurements that drive the σ-proxy caveat.

What's left to prove

Open problems on the table

These are the unresolved items, listed honestly. Solving any one of them would substantially strengthen the framework; failing to solve them would substantially weaken it.

OQ1 — First principles

Derive the universal vacuum kernel

The vacuum response kernel is currently treated as Gret(b) ≈ Gret(0) — an effective approximation. A complete derivation from quantum gravity remains open.

OQ2 — EFT consistency

Derive cs and full EFT

The sound speed cs ~ 20–40 km/s is currently estimated from cs ~ √(gb r). It needs to be derived self-consistently within the effective theory rather than imposed as a parametric estimate.

OQ3 — Boundary validation

Validate threshold across the ladder

The frozen 5 eV threshold has to be re-run cleanly across the galaxy → group → cluster sequence using only systems with directly measured velocity dispersions. The AUC must stay high under that restriction.

OQ4 — σ proxy

Replace the proxy

The galaxy-σ proxy in the directional ladder is the largest remaining caveat. The headline correlation must be reproduced using direct spectroscopic σ before the boundary result can be called final.

OQ5 — Tier 2 program

Lift to first-principles theory

The current framework is Tier 1 (effective field theory / phenomenology). The Tier 2 program — deriving everything from first principles, removing adjustable parameters, and explaining why the vacuum behaves this way — is the long-term direction.
